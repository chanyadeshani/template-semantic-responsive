Social Services and SEND support for families in Swindon

SIAS

This service provide information advice and support for young people from 0-25 with special educational needs and disabilities as well as their parents/carers. All SIAS workers have SEND lega ltraining run by the national information advice and support service. Meaning they gibe legally based and easily accessible information, advice and support about SEND, including your rights and responsibilities and those of schools/colleges etc. They are trained to give accurate, useful anf relevant infomation and advice to empower you to make decisions about your education of children you have responsibility for.

They help with:Explaining legal processess, including education, health and care needs assessments,EHC plans, appeals and tribunals, so that you know what you need to do and when

  • Preparing for meetings and communicating with professionals about you or your child's education
  • Developing a good working relationship with your child's school
  • They can attend meetings with you of the situation is particularly difficult
    • They can help with working out what is important and what to focus on

Contact Swindon

The front door for the council's childrens social care services Contact Swindon is an updated way to contact childrens services and supplies a point of contact to ask for help or report concerns for a child. It is available for everyone in the borough that can access it. Contact Swindon will carry out improved and important safegaurding decisions for children in Swindon as well as young people and their families and support networks. Contact Swindon have locality teams which undertake the requests that come through, they then provide the correct support and make sure it is delivered at the right time.
